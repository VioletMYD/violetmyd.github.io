Showcase of Some of My Favorite Movies

Promare

GENRES Action, Mecha, Sci-Fi, Super Power        RUNTIME 111 minutes        STUDIO Trigger        RATING PG-13

Suddenly, three decades ago, a new breed of fire-wielding mutants emerged, referred to as the "Burnish," obliterating a significant portion of humanity. These gruesome beings continued to surface unpredictably, leaving a trail of catastrophe and death in their wake. Fueled by the leadership of Kray Foresight, the autonomous republic of Promepolis blossomed into a prosperous nation, thanks to his tireless efforts in battling the Burnish. Mechanic Lucia Fex's inventive know-how has equipped firefighting squad Burning Rescue with state-of-the-art technology to combat these abominations. Galo Thymos, a spirited newcomer who idolizes Foresight for saving his life, joined the rescue team. A terrorist faction referred to as Mad Burnish has sown chaos throughout the country. After a confrontation with Mad Burnish's chieftain, Lio Fotia, Galo embarks on a destined journey to uncover the reality concerning these mutants, leaving him to question all he knows to be factual.

Princess Mononoke

GENRES Action, Adventure, Drama, Fantasy, Demons        RUNTIME 133 minutes        STUDIO Studio Ghibli        RATING PG-13

A peaceful mountain village is suddenly attacked by a demon- possessed wild boar, and Ashitaka - a young prince in the tribe - decides to fight it to save his people. Sadly, during the fight, he is wounded on the arm, and the curse slowly begins to spread to his entire body. Following tradition, he is exiled, and as a nomad, he sets out to find a cure before the curse consumes him entirely. Iron Town is a powerful citadel run by Lady Eboshi, famous for exploiting the natural resources of the area - chopping down trees and extracting iron-rich sand to make weapons and machinery. Unfortunately, this destruction of habitats angers the nearby animal tribes, led by San, a young girl from the Wolf clan seeking revenge. Ashitaka arrives in Iron Town to find a violent conflict, and though he despairs at the situation, he is determined to make peace with the Wolf clan. It remains unclear whether the Spirit of the Forest will help Ashitaka break the curse, especially in a world torn apart by brutal conflicts between humans and beasts, and with the forest itself in peril.

given the movie

GENRES Drama, Music, Romance, LGBTQA+        RUNTIME 59 minutes        STUDIO Lerche        RATING PG-13

Movie sequel to the TV show, given, the film centers on Haruki Nakayama, the bassist of the band, Akihiko Kaji, the drummer, and Ugetsu Murata, Akihiko's ex-partner and current roommate, exploring their romantic dynamics.
